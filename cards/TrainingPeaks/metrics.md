Q: What does ATL stand for?
A: Acute Training Load

---

Q: What does ATL measure?
A: Short-term training load, reflecting recent fatigue. It is a rolling average (typically 7 days) of daily Training Stress Score (TSS).

---

C: [ATL] is commonly called "fatigue" in TrainingPeaks.

---

Q: What does CTL stand for?
A: Chronic Training Load

---

Q: What does CTL measure?
A: Long-term fitness, reflecting accumulated training over time. It is a rolling average (typically 42 days) of daily TSS.

---

C: [CTL] is commonly called "fitness" in TrainingPeaks.

---

Q: What does TSB stand for?
A: Training Stress Balance

---

Q: What is the formula for TSB?
A: TSB = CTL − ATL

---

Q: What does TSB represent?
A: Form or freshness. A positive TSB means you are fresher than fatigued; a negative TSB means you carry more fatigue than fitness.

---

C: TSB is commonly called "[form]" in TrainingPeaks.

---

Q: What does TSS stand for?
A: Training Stress Score

---

Q: What is TSS?
A: A single number representing the overall training load of a workout, accounting for both intensity and duration. A 1-hour ride at threshold power equals 100 TSS.

---

Q: What does NP stand for?
A: Normalized Power

---

Q: What is Normalized Power?
A: A weighted average power that accounts for variability in effort, giving a better estimate of the metabolic cost of a ride than simple average power.

---

Q: What does FTP stand for?
A: Functional Threshold Power

---

Q: What is FTP?
A: The highest average power a cyclist can sustain for approximately one hour, measured in watts. Used as the reference intensity for all power-based metrics.

---

Q: What does the PMC display?
A: A chart showing CTL (fitness), ATL (fatigue), and TSB (form) over time, used to plan training peaks and tapers.

---

Q: What does rTSS stand for?
A: Running Training Stress Score

---

Q: How is rTSS calculated?
A: Based on pace, grade, and duration relative to the athlete's threshold pace, analogous to TSS for cycling.

---

Q: What does sTSS stand for?
A: Swim Training Stress Score

---

Q: What does HRV stand for?
A: Heart Rate Variability

---

Q: What is HRV in the context of training monitoring?
A: A measure of the variation in time between heartbeats, used as an indicator of recovery status and readiness to train.

---

Q: What does LTHR stand for?
A: Lactate Threshold Heart Rate

---

Q: What is LTHR?
A: The heart rate at which lactate begins to accumulate in the blood faster than it can be cleared; used as a reference intensity for heart-rate-based training zones.

---

Q: What does VO2max stand for?
A: Maximum Oxygen Uptake

---

Q: What is VO2max?
A: The maximum rate at which the body can consume oxygen during intense exercise, expressed in mL/kg/min. A key indicator of aerobic capacity.

---

Q: What does W/kg stand for?
A: Watts per kilogram

---

Q: Why is W/kg significant?
A: It normalizes power output to body weight, enabling comparison of climbing ability across athletes of different sizes.

---

Q: What does MMP stand for?
A: Mean Maximal Power

---

Q: What is the MMP curve?
A: A graph showing the best average power an athlete has achieved for every duration (e.g., 5 s, 1 min, 5 min, 20 min, 60 min). Also called the power curve or power duration curve.

---

Q: What does TIZ stand for?
A: Time in Zone

---

Q: What is TIZ used for?
A: Tracking how much training time is spent in each heart rate or power zone to ensure the intended training distribution is achieved.

---

Q: What does AeT stand for?
A: Aerobic Threshold

---

Q: What is the aerobic threshold?
A: The intensity below which the body relies primarily on fat for fuel and lactate remains at near-baseline levels. Often called Zone 2.

---

Q: What does AnT stand for?
A: Anaerobic Threshold

---

Q: What is the anaerobic threshold?
A: The intensity at which lactate production exceeds clearance capacity; roughly synonymous with FTP/LTHR. Also called the lactate threshold or VT2.
